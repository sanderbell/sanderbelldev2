## 2. The Problem

Non-technical professionals — therapists, influencers, coaches, designers, teachers, business owners — often have viable product ideas but hit one of three walls:

1. **Blank page paralysis.** No framework for where to start, what to cut, or how to scope v1.
2. **The vibe coding trap.** They try AI tools (Cursor, Lovable, Replit). The first hour feels like magic. Within days: unexplained bugs, screens that don't connect, code they can't fix. They close the laptop. This is a structure failure, not a discipline failure.
3. **The agency black box.** They hire a dev shop for a high fee, wait three months, and receive the easiest interpretation of what they asked for.

The root problem is never the code. It's the judgment layer: knowing what to build, in what order, and why — before anything gets written. That judgment requires someone with skin in the game, someone who has shipped real products and understands both the technical and human sides.

## 7. Why AI Is Not Enough

1. **UX intuition.** AI pattern-matches from existing products. It doesn't understand the emotional context of a specific user in a specific situation.
2. **Multi-version perspective.** AI optimizes for the current prompt. A product builder makes v1 decisions with v3 in mind.
3. **Platform judgment.** Mobile vs web, iOS vs Android, native vs PWA — these determine distribution, cost, and maintenance. AI implements whatever you ask without flagging the consequences.
4. **Scope discipline.** Client excitement generates ideas. AI accommodates all of them. A human kills the ones that sink the timeline.
5. **Trust.** The client is handing over something they've protected privately. AI cannot be trusted with something that matters.
6. **Error diagnosis.** Architectural bugs require pattern recognition from lived shipping experience — not training data.
7. **The mobile infrastructure maze.** Getting AI to generate screens is one thing. Getting a real app to actually run on a phone — through Apple's and Google's technical requirements — is another universe entirely. AI can suggest fixes but cannot reliably navigate the sequence. A wrong step costs days. This layer alone is where most non-technical vibe-coding attempts die.
8. **Translation.** "I want it to feel safe." "I want it to feel like a conversation." AI takes these literally. A human builds them into the product.

## 10. Delivery

- **Day 1 — Discovery:** 60–90 min structured conversation. Problem, user, scope, what's explicitly out of v1. Summary doc sent same day.
- **Days 2–3 — Blueprint:** User flows, feature tiers, screen logic, technical approach in plain language. One feedback round.
- **Days 3–7 — Build:** First version. Blueprint sign-off is the contract. New ideas logged for v2, not incorporated.
- **Days 8–10 — Feedback:** Client tests beta. One revision cycle.
- **Days 11–14 — Handoff:** Final delivery, store submission, handoff playbook, 48hr Q&A, retainer conversation offered naturally.
